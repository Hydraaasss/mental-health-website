How Mental Health Affects Students

Mental Health is a serious issue when it comes to University Students, caused by harsh deadlines, tough work, amount of course work, revision. Whilst also trying to balance a social life, and fit in any of your own hobbies. Mixing all of these together, without a schedule, is going to turn out be chaos. Below, We have listed, some of the key ways mental health can attack students:

Academic Effects

Students struggling with mental health issues often find it difficult to concentrate, complete assignments, or attend classes. This can lead to:

  • Lower grades and academic performance.
  • Increased dropout rates.
  • Difficulty meeting deadlines.
  • Missing Lectures and Labs

Social Effects

Mental health challenges can make it hard for students to connect with others, leading to:

  • Feelings of loneliness and isolation.
  • Strained relationships with friends and family.
  • Difficulty participating in social activities.
  • Being Stuck in your house or accomodation

Emotional Effects

Mental health issues can take a toll on a student's emotional well-being, causing:

  • Increased anxiety and stress.
  • Feelings of hopelessness or depression.
  • Low self-esteem and confidence.
  • Emotionally Detached and Anger
